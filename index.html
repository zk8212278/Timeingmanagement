Time Managment Point

1. Pomodoro Technique

Work in focused 25-minute increments, followed by a 5-minute break. After four cycles, take a longer break (15-30 minutes). This technique can help you stay focused and avoid burnout.

2. Time Boxing

Schedule fixed, uninterrupted blocks of time for tasks. This helps prioritize tasks, avoid multitasking, and make realistic estimates.

3. Avoid Context Switching

Minimize switching between tasks or projects. Context switching can decrease productivity by up to 40%. Try to complete tasks in a single session or limit switches to reduce overhead.

4. Use a "Stop Doing" List

Identify tasks, meetings, or activities that consume time without adding value. Eliminate or delegate them to free up time for high-priority tasks.

5. Schedule "White Space"

Leave buffer time between tasks or meetings for relaxation, learning, or unexpected tasks. This helps reduce stress, increases productivity, and allows for flexibility.
